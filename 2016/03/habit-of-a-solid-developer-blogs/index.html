Blogs played a huge part in my own software development related education. I can't say for sure if they are still a good way to consume development related content. When I first started soaking up blog content back around 2005, I was so green that I absorbed everything I could from sites like Code Better, Los Techies, Elegant Code, and Devlicious. I learned some great topics from these sites such as S.O.L.I.D. principles, unit testing, and others.

I still subscribe to quite a few blogs and often enjoy them when I have some quiet time to sit and consume. However, they play less of a teaching role these days and I tend consume less content as well as switching to more audio-based content such as podcasts.

When consuming blog posts

It's easy to get bogged down in content once you ramp up the number of subscriptions you have.

Did I mention I still use RSS? For some people, this is like a lost technology. However, I still see it as a great way to get access to updated content from some solid blogs.

but learning to skim blog post headlines and skipping over ones that are of no interest is a great way to get through and consume just what's interesting.

RSS blog feeds may not necessarily be the best way to consume anymore as the better content is often times bubbled up on Twitter, Reddit and Hacker News, and others.

I'm currently using Newsify: Your News, Blog & RSS Feed Reader - Ben Alexander to sync my Feedly subscriptions to my iPhone. These posts are great for when you're killing time (say waiting in a Dr. office). This app is great for consuming content offline (like an airplane) or in places I don't feel like listening to a podcast.

Publishing blog posts

I don't have a ton to add here, but to say that I've quite enjoyed writing this blog and the ability to share what I've learned along my journey. While most posts are only seen by few, there are some that get significant number of visits. These make me happy because it shows that there was some serious value in the content and it keeps me motivated to continue the process.

Happy Reading!
