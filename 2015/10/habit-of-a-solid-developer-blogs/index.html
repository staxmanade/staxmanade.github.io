I don't know if this is still a good way to consume development related content anymore. I feel like I really soaked up the blogs when there was some really solid content being put out on sites like Code Better, Los Techies, Elegant Code, and Devlicious.

Consider starting your own blog.

While there are lots of people out there blogging, not everyone is and not all solutions to every problem have been written.

The technology landscape is always changing and there are always opportunities to write about what you're struggling with, learning, or are an expert in.

My most successful blog posts are usually boring/dry topics that cover something that I struggle do to get detailed information on line about. Or ones that have bits/pieces scattered across various blogs, documentation sites, etc. If you can cleanly organize some topic of content, throw a blog post out there you can not only help the next guy coming along looking for help, but it's quite fun to see the visitors to your own blog grow.

I'm currently using Newsify: Your News, Blog & RSS Feed Reader - Ben Alexander to sync my Feedly subscriptions. This app is great for consuming content in places I don't feel like listening to a podcast.

Happy Reading!
